# Debate Forum

Have you ever wished you could discuss a plot twist or character consistency with a team of professional editors? LoneWriter's Debate Forum allows you to invoke different AI agents to analyze your work from multiple perspectives.

---

## How Debate Works

Unlike rewriting, the Debate Forum does not modify your text directly. It is a consultation space where you can:
1. **Select** a fragment of your novel or write a specific question.
2. **Activate** the participants you want (Editor, Critic, Proofreader...).
3. **Start the Debate** and let the AI discuss with each other to offer you a reasoned conclusion.

---

## The Participants (Agents)

LoneWriter includes three pre-configured profiles, each with a distinct "personality" and technical focus:

- **Editor**: Focuses on narrative structure, plot pacing, and character arcs.
- **Critic**: Offers an analytical and honest evaluation, comparing techniques and looking for weak points in the story's logic.
- **Proofreader**: Handles the technical side: grammar, spelling, writing tics, and sentence-level fluency.

---

## Agent Management

You can customize your debate team according to your needs:
- **Add Participants**: Create your own agents with specific "System Prompts" (e.g., a Nordic mythology expert or a scientific advisor).
- **Debate Rounds**: Configure how many times agents should respond to each other to deepen the discussion.
- **Context**: Enable or disable agent access to the current scene or your Compendium so they have more or less background information.

---

## Forum Memory
Each debate chat is saved independently. You can browse your discussion history, rename threads, or delete them when no longer needed.

> [!TIP] TIP
> The Debate Forum is especially powerful when used alongside the Compendium. If you enable Compendium context, agents can warn you if your idea contradicts what you've already written about your world.
